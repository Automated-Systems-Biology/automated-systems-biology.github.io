Summary

The main goal of the course is to give a good general overview of optical methods used in the fields of biological sciences. Biophotonics harnesses the interaction of light and biological matter from the level of single molecules and cells all the way to tissues to together important biological insights. Besides giving students a good foundation in optics, the course will focus on methods like spectroscopy, microscopy, biosensors, sequencing, and the application of fluorescence in biology. The course will have a special emphasis on high-throughput methods and biomedical applications.

Course outline

  1. Geometric Optics I — light rays, the law of reflection, the law of refraction, the axioms of geometric optics; prisms and total internal reflection; images and image formation.
  2. Geometric Optics II — imaging properties of spherical surfaces, image formation by two spherical surfaces, ray tracing; the Lens Maker's Equation and simple multi-lens instruments.
  3. Wave Optics — historic emergence of wave optics, types of waves, the Huygens principle (reflection and refraction); interference in one and two dimensions and two-slit interference; diffraction, single- and two-slit diffraction, the effect of slit width, wavelength and slit distance, and the Airy disk.
  4. Spectroscopy — quantum nature of light; key components of a spectrophotometer (light sources, detectors, monochromators); UV-VIS spectroscopy and the Lambert-Beer law; fluorescence and fluorescence spectroscopy.
  5. Microscopy I: The Microscope — the compound microscope, microscope optics and optical aberrations; resolution, digital image formation, magnification and camera basics.
  6. Microscopy II: Contrasting Methods — contrast in optical microscopy; dark field microscopy, phase contrast microscopy and fluorescent methods.
  7. Microscopy III: Sample Preparation — sample types and processing (fixation, sectioning); imaging and mounting media with emphasis on refractive index matching; live cell imaging.
  8. Biomarkers and Dyes I: Colorimetric Dyes and Methods — colorimetric biomarkers and staining procedures.
  9. Biomarkers and Dyes II: Fluorescent Dyes and Methods — fluorescent biomarkers and staining procedures; introduction to immunoanalytical methods (immunohistochemistry, flow cytometry, ELISA).
  10. Advanced Microscopy Methods — advanced fluorescent microscopy; TIRF and confocal methods; microscopy-based sequencing methods.
