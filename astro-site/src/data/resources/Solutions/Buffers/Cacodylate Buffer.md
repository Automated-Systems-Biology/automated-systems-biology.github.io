# Cacodylate Buffer

## Overview

DESCRIPTION:

Cacodylate buffer's pH range is pH 5-7.4. It is widely used in sample preparation for electron microscopy (eg. in fixation buffers). It is perceived to preserve membrane ultrastructure and does not form microprecipitation on thin sections that can occur in phosphate buffers if the specimen is not well rinsed between pre- and post-fixation. It also does not precipitate in solution with low calcium concentrations. It is easily prepared and has a long shelf life as it does not support the growth of microorganisms and does not precipitate. Its shelflife is over a year especially if refrigerated. Precautions: Contains arsenic and is highly toxic!
