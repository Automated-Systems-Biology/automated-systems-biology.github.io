# Glutaraldehyde

## Overview

DESCRIPTION

Glutaraldehyde is a bifunctional aldehyde crosslinker widely used in biochemistry applications (SDS-PAGE, tissue fixatives. It is usually employed alone or mixed with formaldehyde, paraformaldehyde (see below). Enzyme activity often is preserved making cytochemical localization possible in later steps. Penetrates faster than  osmium tetroxide, but slower than formaldehyde. Exerts some osmotic effect however between 2-5% it is usually negligible. It reacts with some lipids and can cause phospholipids to dissolve (can be prevented 1-3mM CaCl2). It has been shown to be more effective at preserving high-molecular weight DNA than formaldehyde. In general glutaraldehyde is known to deactivate proteins eg. quenching GFP and thus can cause lower immunoreactivity during immunostaining.  Tissues that have been fixed with a glutaraldehyde-based fixative must be treated or quenched with inert amine-containing molecules prior to the IHC staining.The most efficient aldehyde blockers/quenchers are ethanolamine and lysine, Tris-HCl buffer, and ethanolamine. Can also be deactivated with reducing agents like NaBH4.

When clean it is a clear fluid and becomes increasingly yellow due to polymerization. It has to be stored in clean glass vials in the dark and it is stable for many months if stored at -20 °C and may be stored at 4 °C. Typical protocols involve 2-6% glutaraldehyde concentration. In solution (even in dark) glutaraldehyde polymerizes and reacts to form glutaric acid. This results in gradual acidification (thus buffering is needed)(. Sabatini et al. 1967). Glutaraldehyde polymerizes rapidly at high pHs. At pH 8.5 or over, for instance, polymerization occurs so rapidly, even at 4°C, that it is advisable not to mix the buffer and fixative until immediately prior to use. Approximately 50% polymerization of purified glutaraldehyde occurs at 4°C in seven weeks at pH 6.5, in three weeks at pH 7.5, and in six days at pH 8.5. Glutaraldehyde is more stable in Cacodylate Buffers (see Marquardt and Gordon)

2. 1 %Glutaraldehyde-4% Formaldehyde (McDowell-Trump or Trump's fixative):

terials and Methods

The recipe we use to prepare McDowell and Trump

4F:1G fixative (4F:1G) is as follows.

Add, in the order listed:

86 ml distilled water

10 ml 37-40 percent formaldehyde/formalin

4 ml 25 percent glutaraldehyde (biological grade)

1.16 g NaH2PO4.H2O

0.27 g NaOH

Stir while adding components.

Check the pH when done. Some 0.1 N NaOH can

be added to raise the pH if it is below 7.2-7.4.

Once prepared, 4F:1G can be stored at 4oC for three

months. Tissues fixed in 4F:1G can be stored up to 23

years (so far) at 4oC.
