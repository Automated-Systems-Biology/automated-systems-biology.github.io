# Sterilization of Equipment and Media

## Sterilization Protocol

LOADING AUTOCLAVE:

Empty Containers:

1. Fill blue cap glassware, vials, or containers to about 1/3 with ddH2O.
2. Place cap on container BEWARE cap have to be loose.
3. Cover the container top with aluminum foil and stick indicator tape on it.
4. Place in an autoclave.

Liquids:

1. Liquid levels can NOT be filled more than to 4/5-th of total volume.
2. Place cap on container BEWARE cap have to be loose.
3. Cover the container top with aluminum foil and stick indicator tape on it.
4. Place in an autoclave.

Plasticware:

Tubes (eg. eppendorf tubes, storage tubes, caps): have to be placed into a secondary container and covered with aluminum foil. Stick indicator tape onto the aluminum foil.

Pipette tips: tips are placed into autoclavable boxes. Indicator tape is used to close the top.

STERILIZATION:

Reference Container:

1. Take an empty container with the same volume and shape as the largest liquid body being sterilized and fill it with ddH2O to at least to the same level.
2. Cover the mouth of the container with aluminum foil.
3. Cover the cap with aluminum foil and stick indicator foil on it (as this water can be used as sterile water).
4. Place both the container and cap into the autoclave.
5. Stick the reference temperature probe into the container by piercing the aluminum foil.

Autoclave:

1. Place the second temperature probe in the autoclave.
2. Start the appropriate program on the autoclave.

RISKS

Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.
