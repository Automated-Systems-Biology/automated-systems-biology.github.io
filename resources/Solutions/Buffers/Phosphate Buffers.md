# Phosphate Buffers

## Overview

DESCRIPTION:

Phosphate buffer is among the most used buffers in chemistry and biology. These buffers are used in the pH range of  pH 5.8-8. It is Non-toxic to cells and is the most physiological of common buffer salts. It's pH is stable with respect to temperature changes. However, it is prone to getting contaminated with microorganisms. During fixation, phosphate buffers is likely to cause precipitates especially with uranyl acetate and lead salts.   Phosphate Buffer (Sorenson's buffer) pH 5.8-8. Saline containing buffer (PBS,  Dulbecco's phosphate-buffered saline (DPBS),) is an isotonic buffer frequently used in biological applications, such as washing cells. Phosphate buffers bind divalent catios well and precipitate in the presence of alcohol. When sterilizing in an autoclave Mg2+ or Ca2+ ions precipitate so they have to be sterilized separately and combined at room temperature. Sometimes remaining metal ions in phosphate can result in precipitation in an autoclave. Sterilization can be bone by filtration if needed.

## PBS Buffer pH=7.4 (Dulbecco's phosphate-buffered saline or DPBS)

PREPARATION:

Stock solutions:

10x PBS Buffer

Mix the following components in 1L. If needed Calcium and Magnesium ins can be added. For fixed tissue Calcium, Magnesium and K can be ommitted.
