# Buffers

## Phosphate Buffers

DESCRIPTION:

Phosphate buffer is among the most used buffers in chemistry and biology. These buffers are used in the pH range of  pH 5.8-8. It is Non-toxic to cells and is the most physiological of common buffer salts. It's pH is stable with respect to temperature changes. However, it is prone to getting contaminated with microorganisms. During fixation, phosphate buffers is likely to cause precipitates especially with uranyl acetate and lead salts.   Phosphate Buffer (Sorenson's buffer) pH 5.8-8. Saline containing buffer (PBS,  Dulbecco's phosphate-buffered saline (DPBS),) is an isotonic buffer frequently used in biological applications, such as washing cells. Phosphate buffers bind divalent catios well and precipitate in the presence of alcohol

## Citrate Buffers

DESCRIPTION:

Sodium citrate-citric acid buffer is an acidic bufering medium in the range of pH 3-6.2 used for biochemistry and and molecular biology. Widely used in RNA isolation as it prevents base hydrolysis. For the same reason saline Sodium Citrate (SSC) buffer is widely used in in-situ hybridisation reactions. For immunohistochemistry it is said to increace signals without increacing background.

## Cacodylate Buffer

DESCRIPTION:

Its pH range is pH 5-7.4. It is widely used in sample preparation for electron microscopy (eg. in fixation buffers). It is perceived to preserve membrane ultrastructure and does not form microprecipitation on thin sections that can occur in phosphate buffers if the specimen is not well rinsed between pre- and post-fixation. It also does not precipitate in solution with low calcium concentrations. It is easily prepared and has a long shelf life as it does not support the growth of microorganisms and does not precipitate. Its shelflife is over a year especially if refrigerated. Precautions: Contains arsenic and is highly toxic!

## Tris Buffer

DESCRIPTION:

Tris buffers can be used in a biologically relevant pH range of pH=7.1-8.9. It is an organic compound with a zwitterionic structure that intercalates metal ions and inactivates many enzymes (but is still regarded as physiologically inert).  widely used in chemistry and molecular biology especially in agarose and SDS page agarose gel electrophoresis (TBA, TAE etc). It has a good buffering capacity at higher pH required for some tissues. It is important that the buffer pH is temperature dependent.
