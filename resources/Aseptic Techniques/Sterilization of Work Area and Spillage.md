# Sterilization of Work Area and Spillage

## Sterilizing Agents

DISINFECTANT SOLUTION:

1. 70% Ethanol
2. Sodium hypochlorite (Household Bleach):

Stock solution: 20% Bleach Solution

Washin Solution: 2% Bleach Solution

3: Others:

Pathosolv/Molarsolv: proprietary disinfectants containing ethanol, isopropanol, acetone and others.

RISKS

Allways use protective equipment: labcoat, gloves and protective goggles.

## Work Area

PROTOCOL:

Before and after laboratory procedures,  work surfaces must be disinfected.

Table tops can be disinfected using any solvent. Laminar hoods and biological cabinets have to be disinfected with alcohol-based solutions.

Floors have to be regularly cleaned using household bleach solutions.

RISKS

Always use protective equipment: lab coat, gloves, and protective eyewear.

Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.

## Spillage

PROTOCOL:

1. All unnecessary personnel has to be evacuated to safe distance
2. Spillage has to be contained eg. using paper towels
3. 20% Bleach has to be used for primary cleanup.
4. Alcohol-based disinfectants are used for secondary cleanup step 2x!
5. Solids and liquids not in contact with bleach have to be disposed of using waste disposal procedures (see. appropriate protocols).

RISKS

Always use protective equipment: lab coat, gloves, and protective eyewear.

Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.
