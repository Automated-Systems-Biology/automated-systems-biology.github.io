# Waste Disposal

Liquid and Semi Liquid Waste

LIQUID  AND SEMI LIQUID WASTE:

eg. Cell culture media, pre-cultures, culture plates

CHEMICAL DEACTIVATION:

Stock Deactivation Medium:

20% Household Bleach in ddH2O

Protocol:

1. Chemically disinfect with a 1:10 final dilution (vol/vol) of household bleach. In case of multiwell plates fill plate withh 20% bleach.
2. Swirl flask contents and allow a contact time of 30 minutes .
3. Pour down a sink drain and flush the plumbing with an excess of water

Risks:

1. Always use protective equipment, especially laboratory goggles!
2. Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.

PHYSICAL DEACTIVATION:

Protocol:

1. Place culture plates into autoclave bags facing up and stacked to avoid spilage/leakage. Containers with liquid media containing liquids that can be autoclaved can be placed in an autoclave with loosened tops. If needed use secondary containers eg. beakers for glass vial.
2. Place autoclave bags and containers into autoclave but NOT in the basket but the standard bucket
3. Start autoclave program that keeps contents for 30 minutes at 121°C and 200kPa

Risks:

Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.

## Solid Waste

SOLID WASTE: e.g., pipettes, culture flasks or multiple well plates

PROTOCOL:

1. Collect solid biological waste directly into autoclavable bags
2. Close  bag and affix heat-sensitive indicator tape on it. Use a secondary container when needed.
3. Start autoclave program that keeps contents for 30 minutes at 121°C and 15psi
4. Deposit the bag in laboratory waste baskets.

RISKS:

Do not autoclave containers containing bleach. The combination of bleach and residual cotton and oil (improperly cleaned autoclaves) may result in an explosive combustion within the autoclave.
