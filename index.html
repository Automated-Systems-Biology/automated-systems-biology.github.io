Introduction

The Automated Systems Biology Laboratory at Pázmány Péter Catholic University works at the interface of physical chemistry, super-resolution imaging and laboratory automation to study living systems at scale. We design robotics-driven experimental workflows together with the computational tools to analyse them, aiming to make quantitative cell biology more reproducible, higher in throughput and openly shareable. The group is part of the Faculty of Information Technology and Bionics and is led by Dr. Csaba I. Pongor.

Research

We design automated, robotics-driven workflows that bring scale and reproducibility to cell biology. By coupling liquid-handling robots with high-content optical microscopy and computational analysis, we study living systems quantitatively. Recent projects include an automated pipeline that measures competition in mixed yeast colony biofilms from time-lapse imaging, and open-source tools for 3D co-expression analysis in fluorescence microscopy. The group is led by Dr. Csaba I. Pongor, working at the interface of physical chemistry, super-resolution imaging and laboratory automation.

Instrumentation

Our laboratory runs an integrated suite of automated instruments that covers the full experimental pipeline. Liquid-handling robots from Hamilton, Tecan and Opentrons prepare and process samples with minimal human intervention, performing pipetting, aseptic handling and assay set-up around the clock. A Tecan Spark multimode reader provides UV/VIS and fluorescence spectrophotometry, while imaging is built around fully motorized Nikon Ti2 microscopes for widefield and spinning-disc confocal acquisition, complemented by stereo and upright systems. Together these platforms interoperate in reproducible, high-throughput workflows that span sample preparation, measurement and quantitative image analysis.

Teaching

Our teaching spans three main directions: biophotonics and bioimaging, laboratory automation and robotics, and bioanalytics and molecular diagnostics. These graduate courses connect optics, measurement science and automation with hands-on laboratory practice, and are complemented by individual research courses — guided study, independent laboratory work and thesis projects — that let students pursue their own topics in the lab.
