Summary

The course introduces students to the principles and practice of bioanalytics and molecular diagnostics. It begins by refreshing the foundational knowledge of biomolecules and biological organisms needed to understand modern analytical workflows. Building on this, it covers the core bioanalytical and molecular biology methods used in research and diagnostic laboratories, from sample preparation and separation through to nucleic acid amplification, sequencing, and protein-based assays. Students gain both the conceptual background and a practical overview of the techniques that underpin genomics, proteomics, and clinical molecular diagnostics.

Course outline

  1. Refreshing foundational knowledge
  • Biomolecules: carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids
  • Biological organisms: viruses, bacteria, prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
  1. Basic bioanalytical methods
  • Types of centrifugation
  • Other concentration methods
  • Staining techniques
  • Culturing
  • DNA extraction
  • Gel electrophoresis, fingerprinting
  • Restriction enzymes, ligation, gene editing
  • Nucleic acid amplification: PCR, RT-PCR, strand displacement methods (LAMP)
  • Sequencing
  • Genomics, GMO plants and animals, cloning
  • Proteomics, Western Blot, ELISA
